Our Mission

Cyber Safe Bangladesh is a national awareness initiative dedicated to equipping every Bangladeshi citizen with the knowledge and tools to navigate the digital world safely.

From students and professionals to seniors and small business owners — digital safety is everyone's responsibility and right.

⚠️ Know the Risks

Cyber Threats Facing Bangladesh

Understanding what's out there is the first line of defence. These are the most prevalent cyber threats in Bangladesh today.

🎣

Phishing Attacks

Fraudulent messages impersonating banks like Dutch-Bangla or Bkash, government offices, or popular brands to steal login credentials and financial data.

High Risk
💰

Mobile Banking Fraud

bKash, Nagad, and Rocket users are frequently targeted by scammers using fake OTPs, social engineering, and spoofed customer service calls.

High Risk
😡

Online Harassment

Cyberbullying, stalking, and targeted harassment — particularly affecting women and young people on Facebook, TikTok, and Messenger.

Common
🔓

Account Hacking

Weak passwords, credential stuffing, and SIM swapping are used to gain unauthorized access to social media and banking accounts.

Common
📰

Misinformation

False news and fabricated content spread rapidly via Facebook and WhatsApp groups, creating social unrest and eroding public trust.

Widespread
🔒

Ransomware

SMEs and public sector organizations increasingly targeted by ransomware attacks encrypting critical data and demanding payment in cryptocurrency.

Growing
⚖️ Legal Framework

Digital Security Laws in Bangladesh

Bangladesh has enacted strong legislation to combat cybercrime and protect citizens in the digital space. Know your legal protections.

🏛️ Bangladesh Government
🔐 BGD e-GOV CIRT
2023
Act

Cyber Security Act 2023

Replaced the Digital Security Act with a more focused framework targeting cybercrime, data protection, and digital infrastructure security. Reduces some penalties while strengthening enforcement mechanisms.

Current Law
2018
DSA

Digital Security Act 2018

Established the foundational legal framework against digital crimes including unauthorized access, hacking, malware distribution, online defamation, and identity theft.

Superseded 2023
2006
ICT

ICT Act 2006 (Amended 2013)

The pioneering legislation that first addressed electronic crimes in Bangladesh — covering electronic fraud, unauthorized access, and cyber terrorism.

Section 57 & 66
2022
Data

Digital Personal Data Protection

Proposed framework governing collection, storage, and processing of personal data by organizations operating in Bangladesh — aligned with international standards.

🛡️ Stay Protected

Safety Tips for Every Bangladeshi

Practical advice to stay safe — whether you're a student, professional, parent, or business owner.

01

Use strong, unique passwords for every account. Avoid using your name, birthday, or "123456". Use a password manager to keep track.

02

Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all your important accounts — Facebook, email, and banking apps.

03

Never click suspicious links — even if sent by a known contact. Verify before you click, especially for URLs impersonating banks or government sites.

04

Keep your phone, apps, and software updated. Security patches close vulnerabilities that hackers actively exploit.

05

Review your Facebook and social media privacy settings regularly. Limit who can see your phone number, location, and personal information.

06

Be cautious with public Wi-Fi. Avoid accessing banking or sensitive accounts on unsecured networks. Use a VPN when possible.

01

Never share your bKash, Nagad, or Rocket PIN with anyone — including people claiming to be customer service representatives.

02

Always verify the recipient number twice before sending money. Fraudsters exploit hurried transactions involving wrong numbers.

03

Enable transaction notifications and check your balance regularly. Report any unrecognized transactions to your provider immediately.

04

Do not use mobile banking on rooted or jailbroken phones — they lack standard security protections.

05

Download apps only from official app stores. Fake banking apps are used to harvest credentials and intercept OTPs.

06

If you receive an OTP you did not request, immediately change your PIN and contact your mobile banking provider's helpline.

01

Document all instances of online harassment — screenshot, record, and save evidence before reporting or blocking.

02

Use the "restrict" or "block" features on Facebook and Instagram for persistent harassers. You don't have to engage.

03

Report non-consensual image sharing immediately to platform abuse teams and file a complaint under the Cyber Security Act 2023.

04

Contact the Bangladesh Police Cyber Support for Women unit or call 999 — trained officers handle online harassment cases.

05

Be cautious about sharing your location, workplace, or daily routine publicly on social media — this information can enable real-world harm.

06

Support and believe women who report online abuse — social validation is crucial to encouraging victims to come forward safely.

01

Conduct regular cybersecurity training for all staff — human error is responsible for the majority of business data breaches.

02

Implement data backup procedures with off-site or cloud storage. Test your backups regularly so you can recover from ransomware attacks.

03

Restrict access to sensitive data on a need-to-know basis. Use role-based access controls and revoke credentials immediately when staff leave.

04

Verify payment requests via a separate channel before processing. CEO fraud and invoice scams are increasingly targeting Bangladeshi businesses.

05

Register with BGD e-GOV CIRT to receive early threat intelligence and incident response support for critical infrastructure.

06

Develop and test an Incident Response Plan so your team knows exactly what to do if a breach occurs — time is critical.

🚨 Take Action

Report a Cybercrime

Know how and where to report. Acting quickly makes a significant difference in cybercrime investigations.

In an Emergency, Call 999

Bangladesh Police's national emergency line is available 24/7. For cyber-related crimes, ask to be connected to the Cyber Support Unit. You can also visit your nearest police station or file online.

Emergency Hotline
999
Bangladesh Police
1

Document Everything

Screenshot messages, URLs, profiles. Note dates and times. Preserve all evidence before it's deleted.

2

Report to Platform

Use built-in tools on Facebook, Instagram, or TikTok to flag harmful content and accounts immediately.

3

File Police Report

Visit your local police station or report online at police.gov.bd. Bring your documented evidence.

4

Contact CIRT

Report to BGD e-GOV CIRT at cirt.gov.bd for technical incidents involving infrastructure or data breaches.
